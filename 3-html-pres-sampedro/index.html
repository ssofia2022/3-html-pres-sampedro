Danvers is a city that is only 14 miles from Andover which has levels of chromium 6 levels that are higher than the limit. It has 4.4 ppb of chromium 6 when the average amount in the US is 0.03… that’s about 146.7% of the average amount.

There are also many more cities in Massachusetts with pollution in their water, like Lawrence.

Effects of too much chromium 6

While small amounts of chromium 6 are fine, too much can lead to occupational athsma, skin irritation, liver problems, reproductive problems, and other unpleasant consequences. Too much of many other chemicals that can be found in water can also cause some of these effects.
